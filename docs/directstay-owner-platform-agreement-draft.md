# DirectStay Owner / Platform Agreement — Initial Draft

> Draft only for Jaimal/counsel review. Not legal advice. Do not send, present as final, or enable signature flow until explicitly approved.

## 1. Purpose and Parties

This DirectStay Owner / Platform Agreement (the “Agreement”) is between:

- **DirectStay / Operator:** DirectStay or the DirectStay operating entity identified in the final signature record (“DirectStay”), and
- **Owner:** the property owner, authorized representative, or operating entity identified in the final signature record (“Owner”).

The Agreement governs DirectStay’s setup, website, direct-booking support, and related operational services for the vacation rental property or properties identified in the final property record or attached statement of work (each, a “Property”).

## 2. Relationship of the Parties

DirectStay provides technology, website, marketing, operational, and optional booking-support services. DirectStay is not purchasing, leasing, or taking ownership of the Property.

Unless a later owner-approved addendum says otherwise:

- Owner remains responsible for property ownership, legal compliance, guest hospitality, safety, taxes, permits, insurance, and on-site operations.
- DirectStay is an independent service provider, not Owner’s employee, broker, property manager, legal counsel, tax advisor, or insurance advisor.
- DirectStay may describe the Property publicly for guest-facing marketing only as authorized by Owner and this Agreement.

## 3. Services DirectStay May Provide

DirectStay may provide some or all of the following services, as agreed in the proposal, onboarding record, or later written addenda:

1. **Property website and direct-stay presence**
   - Design, build, host, maintain, and improve a guest-facing property website.
   - Create property positioning, page copy, image selections, local guide content, inquiry flows, and conversion-focused updates.
   - Support DirectStay-hosted URLs, property subfolders, and/or custom domain setup.

2. **Lead, inquiry, and reservation support**
   - Capture and organize guest inquiries.
   - Prepare response drafts, quote drafts, reservation records, or follow-up suggestions.
   - Support contract/payment/status tracking where enabled.

3. **Operational support**
   - Maintain owner/admin records, launch checklist, property content, booking terms, guest rules, and internal timelines.
   - Help Owner refine onboarding material, photos, local recommendations, and guest-facing details.

4. **Marketing and performance support**
   - Improve property presentation, local search content, conversion paths, and direct-booking strategy.
   - Recommend experiments or updates based on site/inquiry performance.

Any service not expressly included in the approved proposal or property record is optional and may require separate written approval.

## 4. Preview Builds and Pre-Launch Work

DirectStay may create a preview website or draft materials before launch. Preview Builds are non-final sales/onboarding artifacts unless and until DirectStay and Owner approve launch readiness.

Preview Builds may include assumptions based on public sources or Owner-provided material. Owner must review and correct any inaccurate details before the site is treated as production-ready.

No preview should be treated as a live booking engine, payment system, or final legal representation of the Property unless explicitly approved.

## 5. Owner Responsibilities

Owner agrees to:

- Provide accurate property details, photos/media rights, rates or pricing guidance, house rules, cancellation terms, taxes/fees guidance, permit/licensing details, and guest policies.
- Promptly review and correct draft website copy, booking terms, guest rules, and operational details.
- Maintain the Property in safe, legal, clean, rentable condition.
- Carry appropriate insurance and comply with applicable laws, permits, HOA/community rules, tax obligations, and safety requirements.
- Honor confirmed reservations and guest commitments made through approved DirectStay workflows.
- Respond reasonably to DirectStay requests needed for launch, guest support, or issue resolution.
- Not provide misleading, infringing, unsafe, or unlawful content.

## 6. Commercial Terms, Fees, and Payment Processing

The final proposal or statement of work should specify the commercial structure, which may include one or more of:

- setup fee,
- monthly platform/service fee,
- DirectStay commission or success fee,
- payment-processing or pass-through fees,
- custom launch, maintenance, content, or support terms.

Unless the final agreement states otherwise:

- Payment-processing fees, chargeback fees, tax remittance costs, refunds, and third-party service costs may be passed through to Owner or deducted from amounts payable to Owner.
- DirectStay may invoice Owner, deduct approved fees from collected booking funds, or use another owner-approved payment flow.
- Owner remains responsible for lodging taxes, occupancy taxes, income taxes, licensing, and regulatory reporting unless DirectStay expressly agrees in writing to handle a specific item.

No pricing or fee structure should be presented externally as final until Jaimal has approved the proposal terms.

## 7. Booking Funds, Refunds, Chargebacks, and Taxes

If DirectStay later supports guest payment collection, the operational flow should identify:

- who is merchant of record or payment account owner,
- when funds are collected,
- when Owner receives payouts,
- how DirectStay fees are deducted,
- how refunds, cancellations, disputes, and chargebacks are handled,
- who calculates, collects, and remits applicable taxes.

Until those details are approved in a launch-ready payment addendum, this draft does not authorize DirectStay to take live guest payments for Owner.

## 8. Content Rights and Brand Use

Owner grants DirectStay a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free license during the term of this Agreement to use Owner-approved property names, photos, videos, descriptions, logos, amenities, local recommendations, and related content as needed to provide the services.

Owner represents that Owner has the rights necessary to provide such content and that DirectStay’s authorized use will not violate third-party rights.

DirectStay may create new copy, layouts, strategy, templates, code, automations, and operational artifacts. Unless otherwise agreed:

- Owner may use final approved guest-facing property copy and imagery for the Property.
- DirectStay retains ownership of DirectStay platform code, templates, internal systems, workflows, automations, strategy materials, and non-owner-specific tooling.
- DirectStay may reuse generalized know-how, patterns, and non-confidential learnings.

## 9. Domains, Hosting, and Third-Party Services

The Property may use a DirectStay-hosted URL, an Owner-owned custom domain, or both.

Unless otherwise agreed:

- Owner remains owner of any Owner-owned domain.
- DirectStay may provide DNS/Vercel/hosting setup guidance or manage configuration with Owner approval.
- Third-party providers may have separate terms, outages, fees, and limitations.
- DirectStay may change infrastructure providers if needed to maintain or improve the service.

## 10. Launch Prerequisites

A Property should not be launched as a live direct-booking site until DirectStay and Owner confirm the launch checklist, including as applicable:

- approved property copy, photos, amenities, rules, rates, fees, cancellation terms, and taxes,
- domain/URL and noindex/canonical settings,
- inquiry/booking/payment flow status,
- guest rental agreement and acceptance flow,
- insurance/permit/tax confirmations assigned to Owner,
- support contacts and emergency process,
- Owner approval of final launch state.

DirectStay may refuse or pause launch if required details are missing, inaccurate, unsafe, unlawful, or approval-blocked.

## 11. Data, Privacy, and Confidentiality

Each party may receive confidential or sensitive information from the other, including owner contact details, guest inquiries, reservation details, pricing, operational notes, and property records.

Each party agrees to use such information only for the services, protect it with reasonable care, and avoid unauthorized disclosure except as needed for service providers, legal compliance, payment processing, guest support, or approved operations.

DirectStay may store operational records in its admin systems, audit logs, support tools, and hosting/payment/communications providers as needed to provide and improve the service.

## 12. Guest Communications and Authority

Owner-facing and guest-facing communication authority should be explicit:

- DirectStay may draft owner or guest communications for review.
- DirectStay may send communications only when the approved operating flow authorizes sending or when Owner/Jaimal has explicitly approved the specific communication.
- Owner remains responsible for final property promises, guest eligibility decisions, cancellations, refunds, exceptions, and issue-resolution positions unless DirectStay has written delegated authority.

## 13. Term and Termination

The Agreement begins when signed or otherwise accepted and continues until terminated under the approved terms.

Either party may terminate for convenience with the notice period specified in the final proposal or statement of work. Either party may terminate sooner for material breach if the breach is not cured within the stated cure period, or immediately where continued operation would create legal, safety, fraud, payment, or reputational risk.

Upon termination:

- Owner remains responsible for existing guest commitments unless otherwise agreed.
- DirectStay may remove or disable the DirectStay-hosted site, booking flows, integrations, and admin access.
- Amounts earned or incurred before termination remain payable.
- The parties should coordinate transition of Owner-owned domains, final approved content, and guest/reservation records where legally and operationally appropriate.

## 14. Warranties and Disclaimers

DirectStay will use commercially reasonable efforts to provide the services in a professional manner.

DirectStay does not guarantee specific search rankings, inquiry volume, booking revenue, occupancy, guest behavior, platform uptime, third-party provider performance, legal compliance outcomes, or tax/insurance treatment.

Owner is responsible for reviewing final property representations and obtaining legal, tax, insurance, licensing, and hospitality advice where needed.

## 15. Liability Limits

To the fullest extent permitted by law, neither party will be liable for indirect, incidental, special, consequential, punitive, or lost-profit damages arising from this Agreement.

DirectStay’s total liability should be capped in the final approved version, commonly by reference to fees paid to DirectStay during a defined lookback period, except where such limitation is prohibited by law or excluded by final counsel-approved language.

## 16. Indemnity

Owner should indemnify DirectStay for claims, losses, damages, costs, and expenses arising from:

- Property ownership, condition, safety, maintenance, or operations,
- Owner-provided content or inaccurate property information,
- Owner’s violation of laws, permits, taxes, HOA/community rules, or guest commitments,
- guest injuries, property incidents, or on-site disputes except to the extent caused by DirectStay’s gross negligence or willful misconduct.

DirectStay should indemnify Owner for third-party claims arising from DirectStay’s gross negligence, willful misconduct, or unauthorized use of content outside the scope of this Agreement, subject to final counsel-approved limits.

## 17. Dispute Resolution and Governing Law

The final version should specify governing law, venue, notice addresses, dispute-resolution procedure, and whether mediation, arbitration, jury waiver, class-action waiver, or attorney-fee language is appropriate.

## 18. Signature and Electronic Acceptance

The final operational flow may allow acceptance by:

- e-signature provider,
- typed signature,
- authenticated Owner Portal approval,
- checkbox acknowledgment plus stored agreement version,
- email confirmation if approved by counsel/Jaimal.

The acceptance record should store signer identity, timestamp, agreement version, property/lead context, IP/session metadata where appropriate, and audit timeline events.
