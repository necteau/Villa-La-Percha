# DirectStay Owner / Platform Agreement — Research Notes

> Internal working notes only. Not legal advice. These notes summarize patterns from public platform terms and vacation-rental management agreement guidance so DirectStay can draft its own owner/platform agreement without copying third-party terms.

## Sources Checked

- Lodgify article on short-term rental property management agreements: emphasizes defining parties, duties, term, noncompliance/default, fees, manager/owner responsibilities, and legal review.
- Airbnb Terms of Service/help legal terms: useful pattern categories include incorporated policies, service fees, privacy, off-platform rules, cancellation policy display before booking, refund/rebooking, damage protection, reviews/content policy, community standards, and platform/content licenses.
- Vrbo host terms search result: confirms platform terms commonly govern owner/host use of service, listing properties, limitations of liability, and arbitration/dispute handling. Full fetch was blocked by bot protection, so do not quote or rely on exact language.
- General short-term rental management template results: common clauses are manager duties, owner duties, fee/commission, authority limits, maintenance spending authority, insurance, taxes, booking funds, termination, indemnity, content rights, and dispute resolution.

## Key Clause Families to Cover

1. Parties, property, and scope of relationship.
2. Clear statement that DirectStay is a technology/marketing/operations support provider, not automatically a property manager, broker, real-estate agent, lawyer, tax advisor, insurer, or merchant of record unless an addendum says so.
3. Statement of work / commercial terms snapshot per owner/property.
4. Preview-build limitations and launch-readiness gate.
5. Owner representations: ownership/authority, rights to photos/content, accuracy, permits, taxes, insurance, safety, property condition, legal compliance.
6. DirectStay service commitments: website, inquiry capture, content, lead handling, draft replies, contract/payment tracking, admin records, analytics, and optional guest/booking support.
7. Authority boundaries: DirectStay drafts/recommends; owner or approved workflow controls promises, booking acceptance, prices, refunds, exceptions, cancellations, external sends, and legal/payment actions.
8. Fees: setup, monthly, commission/success fee, pass-through fees, payment-processing/chargebacks/refunds/taxes, late payments, suspension for nonpayment.
9. Booking funds/payment processing: merchant of record, payout timing, deductions, chargebacks, refunds, taxes, payment-provider terms, no live payment collection until addendum/launch approval.
10. Content license and ownership: owner grants rights to use property content; DirectStay owns/reuses platform code, templates, workflows, AI tooling, generalized know-how; owner can use final owner-approved property copy for the property.
11. AI-assisted operations disclosure internally: DirectStay may use AI tools under Bishop/operator supervision for drafts, summaries, triage, content, and workflow support, but external sends/launch/legal terms need approved controls.
12. Data/privacy/confidentiality: guest/owner data, admin/audit logs, service providers, lawful use, breach/security, exported records at termination.
13. Reviews/testimonials: permission-first; no unauthorized copying of OTA reviews/images.
14. Domains/hosting/third-party providers: owner-owned domains stay owner property; DirectStay-hosted URLs and infrastructure can change; third-party outages/terms not guaranteed.
15. Non-exclusivity: decide whether owner may continue Airbnb/VRBO/other channels and whether DirectStay receives commission only on DirectStay/direct bookings. Avoid accidental exclusivity.
16. Channel conflict / OTA compliance: owner remains responsible for respecting OTA terms and avoiding improper off-platform solicitation for OTA-origin guests.
17. Property management boundary: if DirectStay is not doing on-site management, say owner/local manager handles check-in, cleaning, maintenance, emergencies, guest safety, damages, supplies, permits.
18. Launch gate: no live direct booking/payment until property content, terms, guest agreement, payment flow, taxes, support contacts, and owner approval are complete.
19. Term/termination/transition: notice period, cure, immediate suspension for legal/fraud/safety/reputation/payment risk, existing bookings, data/content handoff, domain transfer, survival clauses.
20. Warranties/disclaimers: no guarantee of revenue, occupancy, rankings, guest behavior, third-party uptime, legal/tax outcomes.
21. Liability cap, exclusion of consequential/lost-profit damages, indemnities from owner and DirectStay.
22. Governing law/venue/dispute process: leave for Jaimal/counsel.
23. Electronic acceptance/signature and immutable audit trail.

## DirectStay-Specific Decisions Needed

- Final DirectStay legal entity and notice address.
- Commercial model: setup fee, monthly fee, commission/success fee, hybrid, and whether commission applies to all direct bookings or only DirectStay-attributed bookings.
- Whether DirectStay ever handles guest payment collection or only inquiry/booking support at first.
- If payments are enabled: merchant of record, tax collection/remittance responsibility, refund/chargeback flow, payout schedule.
- Whether DirectStay has permission to send guest communications automatically after owner approves a workflow, or only draft-for-approval at first.
- Whether owner agreement should be non-exclusive by default. Recommendation: non-exclusive unless a property-specific SOW says otherwise.
- Support expectations: hours, emergency boundaries, response-time targets if any.
- Maintenance spending authority: likely none by default; owner/local manager handles.
- Governing law and venue.
