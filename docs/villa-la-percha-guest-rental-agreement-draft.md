# Villa La Percha Guest Rental Agreement — Draft

> Draft only for owner/counsel review. Not legal advice. Do not send as a final or enforceable agreement until Jaimal and/or qualified counsel approves it.

## 1. Parties
This Guest Rental Agreement (the “Agreement”) is between:
- **Owner / Host:** Villa La Percha / property owner or authorized operator identified in the booking record (the “Owner”), and
- **Guest:** the primary booking guest identified in the reservation record (the “Guest”).

The Guest represents that they are at least 25 years old and authorized to enter this Agreement for themselves and all occupants in their party.

## 2. Property
The rental property is **Villa La Percha** together with its furnished accommodations, approved outdoor areas, and any amenities expressly included in the booking confirmation (the “Property”).

## 3. Stay Dates and Occupancy
The reservation dates, check-in date, check-out date, nightly count, approved occupancy, and guest contact details are the dates and details shown in the final booking confirmation.

The Guest agrees:
- not to exceed the approved occupancy,
- not to allow unauthorized overnight visitors if prohibited by the booking terms,
- to ensure all members of the party comply with this Agreement.

## 4. Booking Total and Payment Terms
The total booking amount, payment schedule, and payment method are the amounts and terms shown in the booking confirmation and payment record.

Unless otherwise stated in writing by the Owner:
- a reservation is not considered fully secured until the required payment has been received,
- any remaining balance must be paid by the due date stated in the booking confirmation,
- failure to pay on time may result in cancellation of the reservation, subject to any written exceptions approved by the Owner.

## 5. Cancellations, Refunds, and Changes
Cancellation, refund, rebooking, and date-change treatment will follow the cancellation terms provided to the Guest in the booking flow or booking confirmation.

Any requested change is subject to Owner approval and may require updated pricing, revised stay terms, or a new confirmation.

## 6. Security, Damage, and Additional Charges
The Guest is responsible for the Property during the stay and for any damage, missing items, excessive cleaning, unauthorized occupancy, policy violations, fines, or extraordinary service costs caused by the Guest or anyone permitted on the Property by the Guest.

The Owner may charge or invoice the Guest for documented losses or costs arising from such damage or violations, subject to applicable law and any platform or payment-provider procedures.

## 7. Use of Property and House Rules
The Guest agrees to use the Property in a careful, lawful, and respectful manner.

The Guest and all occupants must comply with all house rules provided before or during the stay, including rules relating to:
- noise and quiet hours,
- smoking or vaping,
- parties, events, and commercial activity,
- pets,
- parking,
- trash handling,
- use of pools, decks, balconies, grills, stairs, or other potentially hazardous areas,
- compliance with local laws, HOA rules, and community standards where applicable.

## 8. Condition, Maintenance, and Access
The Owner will make commercially reasonable efforts to provide the Property in rentable condition for the reserved stay.

The Guest must promptly report any maintenance, safety, or cleanliness issue after arrival.

The Owner or authorized service providers may access the Property when reasonably necessary for emergency response, safety, inspection, maintenance, repairs, legal compliance, or protection of the Property, with reasonable notice when practicable.

## 9. Assumption of Risk
The Guest understands that travel and property use may involve risks, including but not limited to slips, falls, stairs, decks, wet surfaces, weather conditions, power/internet interruptions, and use of recreational or outdoor features.

Except to the extent caused by the Owner’s gross negligence or willful misconduct, the Guest assumes the ordinary risks of occupancy and use of the Property for themselves and all occupants and invitees.

## 10. Liability Limits
To the fullest extent permitted by law, the Owner will not be liable for:
- loss of Guest funds, valuables, vehicles, or personal property,
- injury, delay, inconvenience, or loss caused by utility interruption, weather, force majeure events, third-party service outages, construction nearby, acts of other persons, or conditions outside the Owner’s reasonable control,
- any indirect, incidental, special, or consequential damages.

Nothing in this Agreement limits liability where such limitation is prohibited by law.

## 11. Indemnity
To the fullest extent permitted by law, the Guest agrees to indemnify and hold the Owner and its authorized operators harmless from third-party claims, losses, damages, costs, and expenses arising from the Guest’s breach of this Agreement, unlawful conduct, negligence, or misuse of the Property.

## 12. Default and Removal
If the Guest materially breaches this Agreement or house rules, the Owner may require corrective action, cancel the stay, deny continued access, or seek removal from the Property to the extent permitted by law.

In such a case, refunds are not guaranteed and will be handled according to the Agreement, booking terms, and applicable law.

## 13. Force Majeure
The Owner will not be responsible for failure or delay caused by events outside reasonable control, including severe weather, natural disaster, epidemic, governmental restrictions, utility failure, labor disruption, war, civil disturbance, or similar events.

If occupancy becomes impossible or unlawful due to such an event, the parties will work in good faith on any legally required remedy or any alternative accommodation, credit, date change, or refund the Owner elects to offer.

## 14. Governing Law and Disputes
This Agreement will be governed by the law specified in the final owner-approved booking terms or, if none is specified there, the law chosen by the Owner in the final approved contract version.

The final reviewed version should also specify venue, dispute-resolution procedure, and any mediation/arbitration language if the Owner chooses to include it.

## 15. Entire Agreement and Priority of Records
This Agreement, together with the booking confirmation, payment record, house rules, and any written addenda approved by the Owner, forms the full agreement between the parties for the stay.

If there is a conflict between this draft agreement and the final booking confirmation, the Owner-approved final booking record and final approved agreement language control.

## 16. Electronic Acceptance
The Owner may present this Agreement for electronic review and acceptance.

The final operational flow may allow acceptance by one or more of the following:
- checkbox acknowledgment in the DirectStay booking flow,
- typed signature,
- e-signature provider,
- approval through an owner-approved guest portal or email confirmation flow.

The acceptance record must be stored with the reservation timeline before the reservation is treated as contract-complete.

## 17. DirectStay Operational Notes for Later Integration
These implementation notes are not guest-facing final terms, but should guide later product work:
- Contract status should be visible in the owner/admin reservation timeline.
- Guest acceptance timestamp, signer identity, IP/session metadata, and final contract version should be stored.
- Booking confirmation should clearly show whether contract review/acceptance is still pending.
- Payment-confirmed reservations should still distinguish between payment state and contract-complete state if both are required.
- Final guest-facing copy should be plain-language and mobile-friendly.

## 18. Owner/Counsel Review Questions
Before approval, the Owner and/or counsel should confirm:
1. Governing law and venue.
2. Whether a separate security deposit or hold is required.
3. Exact cancellation/refund language.
4. Age minimum and occupancy thresholds.
5. Any mandatory house-rule clauses specific to Villa La Percha.
6. Whether arbitration, waiver, or forum-selection clauses should be included.
7. Whether local jurisdiction requires additional disclosures or tax language.
