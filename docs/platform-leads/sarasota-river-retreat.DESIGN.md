## Design thesis

Sarasota River Retreat should feel like a quiet, owner-cared Gulf river home, not a glossy beach resort or a generic Florida rental template. The preview’s job is to create planning confidence around a small waterfront stay: owner attention, interior trust, pool/spa questions, dock/kayak rules, firepit use, and local recommendations that still need owner confirmation.

Because the source imagery is thin, the safest internal render direction is deliberately text-forward. Use the confirmed living-room image as a trust/interior card only; do not imply it proves river, pool, dock, kayak, firepit, wildlife, or beach access claims.

## Visual language

- **Mood:** calm river morning, warm owner care, practical confidence, soft Gulf light.
- **Palette:** river teal, mangrove green, morning sky, warm shell, muted pool blue, small firepit amber accents.
- **Type rhythm:** warm editorial display for the promise; humanist sans for planning details and owner-confirmation notes.
- **Shape/spacing:** rounded cards, generous whitespace, soft section breaks, avoid dense OTA/listing grids.
- **Imagery:** text-first hero; one living-room trust image in the owner-care/interior section; all amenity sections text-only until a rights-safe exterior/gallery set exists.

## Page rhythm

1. Text-forward hero: quiet Sarasota river retreat + owner details guests need.
2. Stay-fit strip: 3 bedrooms, sleeps 6, Sarasota/Phillippi River context, pool/spa/dock/kayaks/firepit as source-observed but rules-confirmation-needed.
3. Owner-care/interior trust: use the living-room image here only.
4. River/pool/dock day rhythm: text-only, with every operational amenity framed as owner-confirmation-needed.
5. Read-only date/inquiry mock: ask about dates, party size, water/kayak expectations, pool/spa/firepit use, children/mobility, and local guidance needs.
6. Structure-only trip-total clarity: no numeric savings or rate claims.
7. Local planning: categories only until owner recommendations and distances are confirmed.
8. Owner-correction panel: rights, rules, rates, taxes/fees, availability, cancellation, safety, and local details.

## Copy tone

Use plain, careful planning language:

- “quiet river rhythm,” “owner-cared,” “planning confidence,” “confirm before guests commit”
- “pool/spa rules owner-confirmation-needed,” “dock/kayak use to verify,” “local recommendations placeholder”

Avoid:

- “luxury waterfront estate,” “guaranteed wildlife,” “walk to beach,” exact drive times, exact savings, best-rate claims, boating/swimming permissions, or named restaurants/beaches unless sourced and owner-approved.
