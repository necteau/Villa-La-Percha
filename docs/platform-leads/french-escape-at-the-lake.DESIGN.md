## Property fingerprint

- **Property type:** Lake Norman lakefront group/family retreat near Charlotte.
- **Guest promise:** A practical lake-house base where groups can understand sleeping layout, lake-day rhythm, shared spaces, and direct-inquiry details before committing.
- **Known capacity/story:** Source says sleeps up to 12, 4 bedrooms, 3 bathrooms, two kitchens, game room, screened patio, hot tub, kayaks, private dock, direct water access, fire pit near the dock.
- **Primary uncertainty:** Exact location, dock/boat/PWC rules, swim/water safety, parking, event/noise limits, hot tub rules, pet policy, availability, rates, fees, and local distances.

## Visual direction

### Palette

- **Lake dusk blue/green:** pull from water and shoreline evidence; use for quiet section backgrounds and small UI accents.
- **Warm dock/sunset neutrals:** use amber/tan for inquiry and date-planning moments; should feel like late-afternoon lake time, not luxury resort gold.
- **White kitchen + soft stone:** keep cards and text areas light, practical, and clean to echo the interiors.
- **Fireplace charcoal/stone:** use sparingly for headings/dividers and to ground the page.

### Typography and rhythm

- Headings: warm editorial serif or softened display face; avoid ultra-luxury fashion styling.
- Body/UI: highly readable sans with generous line-height.
- Rhythm: horizontal, calm, and practical — lake-wide imagery, then compact planning modules. Avoid dense SaaS dashboard cards.

### Image plan

1. **Hero:** exterior/lake-house sunset image if crop keeps the property and lake context clear.
2. **House rhythm:** living room/fireplace/lake-view image for gathering confidence.
3. **Lake access:** dock/lake view image, paired with confirmation-needed language about rules.
4. **Group logistics:** kitchen or sleeping/layout-supporting image if available; otherwise text-only facts.
5. **Amenities:** hot tub at night only as secondary supporting image; avoid overstating capacity/rules.

Reject social icons, OTA logos, platform badges, promotional graphics, text-overlay cards, or unrelated Lake Norman filler.

## Page structure

1. Hero: “Lake Norman group stays, easier to plan direct.”
2. Stay-fit strip: sleeps 12 / 4 bedrooms / 3 baths / dock / hot tub / kayaks / two kitchens — each marked as source-observed for internal benchmark.
3. House rhythm: morning kitchen/lake view, dock day, screened-patio dinner, game/movie night.
4. Sleeping/layout confidence: explain known bedroom layout plainly; include bunk weight note only as practical detail if source-backed and relevant.
5. Read-only date module: dates, party size, children, boat/Jet Ski, celebration type, accessibility/parking needs, and owner-confirmation questions.
6. Illustrative price clarity: direct-stay trip-total example with no guaranteed savings or best-rate claim.
7. Lake Norman planning guide: grocery arrival, marinas/boat logistics, waterfront food categories, Charlotte rainy-day backup; avoid exact recommendations/distances unless verified.
8. Owner callouts: what DirectStay learned, what needs correction, what would be collected before launch.

## Copy rules

- Guest-facing copy should sound like a vacation-rental page, not a DirectStay strategy note.
- Prefer concrete planning language: “tell us if you are bringing a boat” over “unlock direct-booking value.”
- Do not claim live booking, live pricing, live availability, payment, guaranteed savings, platform affiliation, or legal/safety compliance.
- Do not copy owner/site prose verbatim; use it as source inspiration and rewrite in DirectStay’s voice.
- Any sentence that could fit another lake house unchanged should be rewritten with French Escape-specific evidence or removed.

## Owner-correction callouts to include

- Confirm dock, boat, Jet Ski, kayak, swim, and safety rules.
- Confirm hot tub rules, quiet hours, event/celebration boundaries, parking, pet policy, and accessibility constraints.
- Confirm exact sleeping layout, bunk limit, guest count, fees, taxes, and cancellation terms.
- Confirm preferred local recommendations and whether any direct-booking price comparison may be shown.
