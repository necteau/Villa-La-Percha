# Preview Build Guest Copy QA Checklist

Purpose: a fast final scrub before a DirectStay Preview Build is scored, sent to Jaimal for approval, or shown to an owner. This checklist turns the Asheville/Savannah benchmark failure mode into an operator-visible gate: conversion modules can be present and still fail if the public copy sounds like an internal DirectStay memo.

Use after source extraction and first render, before rubric scoring.

## 1. Source-truth sweep

Mark each guest-facing claim as one of:

- **Observed/source-backed** — visible in owner-provided material or public listing text/images.
- **Owner-confirmed** — supplied directly by owner/operator.
- **Draft assumption** — allowed only if framed as a confirmation need, not stated as fact.
- **Unsupported** — remove or move to owner questions.

Hard-fail examples:

- exact distances/walk times without a source;
- safety/accessibility/pet/dock/parking claims without confirmation;
- best-rate or guaranteed-savings language without approved rates and fee math;
- copied OTA reviews, guest names, or testimonial language without permission.

## 2. Public-page relevance sweep

Every public paragraph should do at least one guest or owner-commercial job:

- help the guest understand the stay rhythm;
- reduce inquiry friction around dates, party size, arrival, parking, pets, or amenities;
- explain direct-booking value safely;
- make the destination/micro-location easier to picture;
- surface a question the owner needs to correct before launch.

Move strategy/process notes to owner callouts or artifacts. Delete copy whose only job is to say DirectStay makes a better brand/site.

## 3. Anti-AI / anti-template sweep

Rewrite or delete any sentence that could survive unchanged on another property by swapping the name.

Watchlist phrases:

- elevated experience;
- seamless stay;
- curated guide;
- memorable property story;
- unlock direct booking;
- beyond the marketplace;
- standalone brand;
- lower-friction experience;
- thoughtfully designed retreat;
- perfect basecamp, unless the surrounding details are property-specific.

Replacement pattern: use concrete nouns from the source material — rooms, views, deck/patio/dock, trail/beach/town approach, arrival sequence, sleeping layout, gathering spots, weather/rainy-day backups.

## 4. Conversion-trio voice sweep

The required modules must read like rental-site planning help, not product strategy.

### Calendar/date module

Good: asks for property-relevant date context.

- mountain cabin: long weekend, leaf season, hot-tub/fireplace season, road/arrival confidence;
- city stay: event weekend, arrival window, parking/access, walkable itinerary;
- lake/beach family house: summer week, holiday weekend, check-in day, children, dock/beach equipment questions.

Bad: explains why DirectStay puts calendars near inquiry forms.

### Price/savings module

Good: shows a labeled sample stay pattern and owner-visible assumptions.

Bad: promises best price, guaranteed savings, or broad marketplace fee claims without approved data.

### Area guide

Good: organized as guest routes/rhythms with source-backed or confirmation-needed labels.

Bad: generic attraction list, invented restaurant recommendations, or unsupported walkability claims.

## 5. Section-fit decision

For each questionable sentence, choose one destination:

- guest page;
- owner callout;
- fact register;
- assumption register;
- Jaimal/operator note;
- delete.

If a paragraph mixes guest copy and internal strategy, split it. Do not let internal process language leak into `?view=guest`.

## Exit gate

A Preview Build should not score above **3/5** for copy quality if guest view contains:

- internal words like benchmark, source description, artifact, rubric, strategy, owner-acquisition, or conversion system;
- obvious generic AI filler;
- unsupported concrete claims;
- DirectStay process rationale masquerading as guest copy.

Record the checklist result in the rubric review or benchmark log before marking a preview owner-reviewable.
