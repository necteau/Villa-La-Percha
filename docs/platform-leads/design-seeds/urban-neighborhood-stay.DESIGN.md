# Overview

Urban stays should sell neighborhood access, ease, culture, food, transit/walkability, and the interior’s role as a calm base.

## Colors

Extract from building materials, street context, interior accents, neighborhood signage, and day/night photo mood.

## Layout

- Editorial neighborhood modules.
- Map/local cards.
- Sharper grids and more energetic type when appropriate.
- Clear practical confidence around transit, parking, stairs/elevator, noise, workspace, safety where known.
- Put the date-window mock in event/weekend language: arrival/departure timing, self check-in, parking or loading, high-demand weekends, and short-stay flexibility.
- Keep price comparison copy conservative for city stays; avoid implying guaranteed event-weekend savings, and move rate/fee/tax/platform assumptions into owner notes.
- Area guides should be itinerary-based instead of list-based: first-night walk, morning coffee, landmark route, rainy-day/cultural options, day trips, and what needs owner confirmation.

## Benchmark watchouts

The Savannah carriage-house benchmark showed that urban previews can sound polished while still being too generic. Use source-backed micro-positioning — street/district, river/square/park relationship, parking/access, pool/day-trip rules where applicable — and strip phrases like "source description" or internal research language from guest-facing copy.

## Do's and Don'ts

Do: make location useful and sensory.
Don't: use generic city stock-photo energy or hide practical details.
