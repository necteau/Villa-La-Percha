# Overview

Use this seed for Gulf Coast river, canal, bayou, or quiet waterway homes where guests are buying a private outdoor rhythm as much as a beach trip: coffee by the river, pool/spa time, dock/kayak questions, local restaurant guidance, and a cared-for owner-direct stay.

This is **not** the generic coastal-villa seed. Avoid open-ocean turquoise, resort gloss, and beach-club language unless the actual photos and source facts support it. The Sarasota River Retreat intake showed the need for a calmer riverfront archetype: soft waterway light, owner care, private pool/dock confidence, and careful local/wildlife claims.

## Colors

Derive the palette from actual photos first:

- river/canal/mangrove water tones before ocean blues;
- pale Gulf Coast morning sky and shaded deck neutrals;
- pool/spa blue only if the pool is visually central;
- warm shell, stucco, wood, firepit, or sunset accents;
- interior/kitchen colors from source photos, not Florida stereotypes.

Avoid Miami neon, generic SaaS blue/gray, and saturated beach turquoise unless the property is truly beach-forward.

## Layout

- Lead with a quiet waterway promise: deck/dock/pool/spa/private outdoor rhythm.
- Put owner trust early if source-backed: maintenance care, stocked details, direct owner availability, local recommendations.
- Sequence the page like a stay day: morning river/deck → kitchen/living confidence → pool/spa/dock/kayak day → firepit/sunset reset → date/inquiry questions → trip-cost clarity → local guide.
- Keep first-screen images distinct: waterway or outdoor hero, then an interior/practical confidence image, then a different pool/dock/kayak/firepit image. Do not repeat the same river/pool shot across hero and early sections.
- Use text-only sections when local imagery is not owner-approved or rights-safe.

## Conversion modules

### Calendar/date-window

Use river-retreat inquiry language, not generic availability copy:

- preferred dates and party size;
- whether guests expect mostly pool/spa time, kayaking/dock use, beach outings, restaurant guidance, or quiet river time;
- children, mobility, parking, and water-safety needs;
- pool/spa heating/use questions and firepit/kayak/dock rules marked owner-confirmation-needed;
- read-only preview status visible near the module.

### Price / trip-total clarity

Frame direct booking around clarity and owner relationship, not unsupported savings:

- if the source mentions no Airbnb/OTA fees, keep it owner-confirmation-needed before guest-facing numeric claims;
- use structure-only comparison until rates, taxes, cleaning, deposits, platform fees, pool/spa/kayak/pet/event fees, and cancellation terms are approved;
- avoid "best rate," "lowest price," guaranteed savings, or tax/fee specifics unless owner-approved.

### Area guide

Organize the local guide around practical guest planning:

- river mornings, kayak/weather/tide considerations, dock and wildlife expectations;
- owner-favorite restaurants/coffee/sunset spots as placeholders until confirmed;
- beach-day planning without claiming exact drive times or access unless sourced;
- rainy-day Sarasota/Gulf Coast options when relevant;
- pool/spa/firepit safety and quiet-hours confirmation needs.

Use wildlife copy carefully. "Herons and manatees are part of the owner-described setting" is safer than implying guaranteed sightings.

## Photo-integrity watchouts

Gulf river/canal sources may include lifestyle stock, beach attraction images, wildlife shots, social badges, or repeated pool/water crops. Render only actual property photos, property views, or clearly relevant rights-safe local context. If gallery extraction is incomplete, do not render a shareable preview; first perform a browser/page-order image inventory.

Reject or move to owner-confirmation notes:

- unverified beach/local photos;
- wildlife images that imply guaranteed sightings;
- promotional badges, OTA logos, review cards, or social graphics;
- repeated pool/water hero crops used as filler;
- exact distance, boating, swimming, kayak, pet, event, or accessibility claims not backed by source/owner confirmation.

## Do's and Don'ts

Do: make the stay feel personally cared-for and easy to plan.
Do: distinguish river/canal privacy from a generic Gulf beach vacation.
Do: surface water/pool/dock/kayak/firepit rules as helpful owner questions, not promises.
Don't: use Lake Norman group-house density unless the home is actually a large group retreat.
Don't: over-luxury the property when the owner voice is warm, practical, and maintenance-focused.
Don't: name restaurants, beaches, sunset spots, or drive times until sourced or owner-confirmed.
