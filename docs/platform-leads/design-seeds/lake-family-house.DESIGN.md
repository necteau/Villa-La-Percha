# Overview

Lake/family homes should balance emotion and logistics: easy days together, sleeping clarity, dock/water confidence, activities, meals, rainy-day comfort.

## Colors

Use actual water tone, dock/deck wood, sunset, grasses, upholstery, and exterior paint. A Northern lake cabin should not look like a Florida lake house.

## Layout

- Clear family-flow structure: arrival, groceries/parking, sleeping zones, kitchen/table, dock/deck, kids/rainy-day reset.
- Strong sleeping/amenity confidence, with child/family logistics visible before the inquiry path.
- Distinct first-screen image rhythm: dock/water or shoreline hero, then a different interior gathering image, then a practical confidence image such as game room, bedroom, kitchen, deck, or parking. Do not repeat the same lake/dock shot across the hero and first sections.
- Activity cards tied to the specific lake/town, but keep local/marina/restaurant imagery text-only unless there is a rights-safe source.
- Warm direct-booking language around planning and host knowledge.

## Conversion modules

### Calendar/date-window

Use lake-week language, not generic availability copy:

- summer week, holiday-weekend, and shoulder-season date-window examples;
- check-in/check-out day assumptions marked for owner confirmation;
- party size, children, dock/boat questions, and water-equipment plans collected before inquiry;
- read-only preview status visible near the module.

### Price / trip-total clarity

Frame direct booking as clearer family-trip economics, not guaranteed savings:

- use a 5–7 night example when numbers are illustrative;
- separate lodging, cleaning/owner fees, taxes, and marketplace/service-fee assumptions;
- exclude or explicitly mark pet, equipment, boat, dock, pool/hot-tub, heating, or damage/security-deposit assumptions unless source-backed;
- avoid “best rate,” “lowest price,” or any guaranteed-savings language.

### Area guide

Organize the local section around guest routes and lake logistics:

- lake-day rhythm: dock use, swimming/boating rules, shade, gear storage, cleanup;
- arrival rhythm: grocery stop, parking, keypad/check-in, marina/ramp questions;
- family options: waterfront restaurants, coffee/breakfast, rainy-day Lake Norman/Charlotte-region backups;
- owner-confirmed safety/rules: dock rights, boat/PWC rules, water depth, life jackets, children, pets, quiet hours, HOA/community limits.

## Photo-integrity watchouts

Lake listings often mix property photos with drone shots, promotional cards, local attraction graphics, and repeated sunset/water images. Render only actual property photos, property views, or clearly relevant rights-safe local context. If there is no trustworthy section image, make the section intentionally text-only and record the gap as an owner photo/input request.

## Do's and Don'ts

Do: show how the stay works for families/groups across a real lake day.
Do: make dock/water safety and rules visible as owner-confirmation needs instead of implied promises.
Don't: over-luxury the property if its charm is casual togetherness.
Don't: use broad “Lake Norman lifestyle” filler when exact dock rights, neighborhood, marina access, and owner favorites are still unknown.
